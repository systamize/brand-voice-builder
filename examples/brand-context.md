## Brand

**Barometer** — customer feedback software that turns NPS results into a prioritised action list for SaaS teams.

---

## Reader Context

**Who they are**
Customer success managers and product leads at B2B SaaS companies — typically 20 to 200 people. They own retention numbers. They are not new to feedback tools; they have worked with spreadsheet trackers, point-in-time surveys, and enterprise platforms. They know what NPS is and do not need it explained.

**What they already believe before they find this brand**
They believe they have more data than they can act on. They have seen NPS scores rise and churn rise at the same time, and they know the score is not the problem. They are sceptical of any new tool that promises insight — they have sat through demos of dashboards and left without knowing what to do differently. They are looking for precision, not volume.

**What they are trying to accomplish**
They want to walk into a product meeting with one clear recommendation — backed by actual customer language, not a trend line. Not a report. A recommendation.

**What would make them dismiss the brand immediately**
Positioning that addresses them as buyers rather than practitioners. Copy that takes four sentences to describe a problem they identified three years ago. Features framed as capabilities rather than outcomes. Any mention of "360-degree view" or "holistic feedback management."

**What Claude must understand before drafting**
This reader has been disappointed by tools that promised to surface insights and delivered filters on a graph. They will not be impressed by coverage. They will be won over by specificity — one concrete claim about what changes, with nothing hedged. Warmth here is precision. If the copy respects their time and their experience, they will feel it. If it explains things they already know, they will leave.

---

## Positioning

Barometer does not report on customer sentiment. It produces a prioritised action list. Where other tools show the number and leave the interpretation to you, Barometer names the one thing worth fixing next — in the customer's own words.

---

## Reference styles

**Clear and authoritative** — the tone of a knowledgeable colleague who has already read the room, identified the problem, and is not going to warm up before getting to it.

---

## What this brand stands for

- One precise action is worth more than a complete picture
- Customer language is more useful than customer data — exact words matter, aggregate scores don't
- Respect for the reader's experience and time, shown through specificity rather than warmth

## What this brand stands against

- Dashboard proliferation — more charts, same uncertainty
- Enterprise abstraction — making simple operations look like strategy
- Metric obsession disconnected from a next move
